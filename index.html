Anxiety

Anxiety can feel overwhelming, but there are practical, grounded techniques that many people find helpful. This is not medical advice, just general strategies you can experiment with to see what resonates.

Tips to help:
  • Talk to someone you trust. Sharing what you’re feeling can lighten the load.
  • Slow breathing: Try inhaling for 4 seconds, exhaling for 6. Longer exhales help your nervous system settle.
Depression

Depression can feel heavy and isolating, and reaching out for ways to cope is a strong step.If things ever feel overwhelming, talking with someone you trust or a professional can make a real difference.

Tips to help:
  • Low‑pressure activities: Listening to music, watching a comfort show, or sitting outside can create small moments of relief.
  • Challenge all‑or‑nothing thoughts: Depression often paints everything in extremes. Try replacing “nothing will help” with “maybe one small thing could help a little.”
Imposter Syndrome

Imposter syndrome can sneak up on people who are actually doing really well, which is part of what makes it so frustrating. The goal isn’t to “eliminate” it but to learn how to recognize it and loosen its grip.

Tips to help:
  • Shift from perfection to progress. Ask: “What did I move forward today?” even if it’s tiny.
  • Celebrate effort, not identity. “I worked hard on this” is more grounding than “I’m good at this.”
Bipolar Disorder

Bipolar disorder can be incredibly challenging, and looking for ways to manage it shows real strength.I can share general strategies that many people find grounding and supportive alongside professional care.

Tips to help:
  • Build gentle routines: Predictability helps your brain feel anchored.
  • Grounding techniques: Deep breathing, sensory grounding, or focusing on one task at a time can help during intense moments.
